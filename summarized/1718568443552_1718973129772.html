Exam Preparation Plan: Business Concepts

This is a detailed preparation plan spanning 4 weeks, focusing on the provided business concepts. Each week will focus on specific topics and include recommended research resources.

Week 1: Business Environments and Strategies

  • Topic: Understanding the Legal and Regulatory Framework
    • Focus: Consumer Protection Act (CPA), Employment Equity Act (EEA), Basic Conditions of Employment Act (BCEA), Skills Development Act (SDA), Compensation for Occupational Injuries and Diseases Amendment Act (COIDA), Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment Act (BBBEE).
    • Activities:
      • Read the relevant Acts and regulations online.
      • Identify key provisions and their implications for businesses.
      • Create a summary table comparing the different Acts and their provisions.
      • Resources:
        • CPA: https://www.gov.za/documents/consumer-protection-act-68-2008
        • EEA: https://www.gov.za/documents/employment-equity-act-55-1998
        • BCEA: https://www.gov.za/documents/basic-conditions-employment-act-75-1997
        • SDA: https://www.gov.za/documents/skills-development-act-97-1998
        • COIDA: https://www.gov.za/documents/compensation-occupational-injuries-diseases-amendment-act-130-1993
        • BBBEE: https://www.gov.za/documents/broad-based-black-economic-empowerment-act-53-2003
  • Topic: Analysing the External Environment
    • Focus: PESTLE Analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental)
    • Activities:
      • Choose a company or industry and conduct a PESTLE analysis.
      • Identify opportunities and threats arising from each factor.
      • Analyze the impact of the identified factors on the chosen company or industry.
      • Resources:
        • https://www.mindtools.com/commsskills/pestle-analysis.htm
        • https://www.business-to-you.com/pestle-analysis/
  • Topic: Formulating Business Strategies
    • Focus: Porter's Five Forces Model, Intensive Strategies, Diversification Strategies.
    • Activities:
      • Apply Porter's Five Forces Model to a specific industry.
      • Identify the competitive forces and their impact on the industry.
      • Analyze different intensive strategies (market penetration, market development, product development) and their applicability.
      • Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of diversification strategies.
      • Resources:
        • Porter's Five Forces: https://www.mindtools.com/commsskills/porters-five-forces.htm
        • Intensive Strategies: https://www.business-to-you.com/intensive-strategies/
        • Diversification Strategies: https://www.business-to-you.com/diversification-strategies/

Week 2: Business Operations and HR Management

  • Topic: Employment and Recruitment
    • Focus: Employment contracts, salary determination methods, recruitment methods.
    • Activities:
      • Draft an employment contract template highlighting essential elements.
      • Compare and contrast different salary determination methods (piecemeal, time-related).
      • Research and analyze various recruitment methods (newspaper advertising, interviews).
      • Resources:
        • Employment Contracts: https://www.labourguide.co.za/employment-contracts/
        • Salary Determination Methods: https://www.indeed.com/career-advice/finding-a-job/salary-determination-methods
        • Recruitment Methods: https://www.indeed.com/career-advice/finding-a-job/recruitment-methods
  • Topic: Employee Benefits and Leave Provisions
    • Focus: Fringe benefits, leave provisions as outlined in the Basic Conditions of Employment Act (BCEA).
    • Activities:
      • Create a list of common fringe benefits offered by companies.
      • Research the minimum leave entitlements as defined in the BCEA.
      • Analyze the impact of leave provisions on business operations.
      • Resources:
        • Fringe Benefits: https://www.indeed.com/career-advice/compensation/fringe-benefits
        • BCEA Leave Provisions: https://www.labourguide.co.za/basic-conditions-of-employment-act/
  • Topic: Introduction and Placement of Employees
    • Focus: Induction process, placement procedures, employee skills development.
    • Activities:
      • Develop an induction program for new employees covering key aspects.
      • Analyze different placement procedures for filling job vacancies.
      • Identify strategies for employee skills development.
      • Resources:
        • Induction Program Development: https://www.mindtools.com/commsskills/induction-training.htm
        • Placement Procedures: https://www.indeed.com/career-advice/finding-a-job/placement-procedures
        • Employee Skills Development: https://www.shrm.org/resourcesandtools/hr-topics/talent-acquisition/pages/employee-development.aspx

Week 3: Quality Management and Business Sectors

  • Topic: Quality Management Systems and Practices
    • Focus: Total Quality Management (TQM), PDCA Model, quality circles, quality indicators.
    • Activities:
      • Explain the principles of TQM and its application in businesses.
      • Illustrate the PDCA cycle and its use in quality improvement.
      • Describe the role and benefits of quality circles in organizations.
      • Identify relevant quality indicators for different business functions.
      • Resources:
        • TQM: https://www.quality-one.com/glossary/total-quality-management
        • PDCA Model: https://asq.org/quality-resources/pdca-cycle
        • Quality Circles: https://www.quality-one.com/glossary/quality-circle
        • Quality Indicators: https://www.isixsigma.com/tools-templates/quality-management/quality-indicators/
  • Topic: Understanding Different Business Sectors
    • Focus: Primary, secondary, and tertiary sectors.
    • Activities:
      • Identify examples of businesses operating in each sector.
      • Analyze the interdependencies between different sectors.
      • Discuss the challenges and opportunities facing each sector.
      • Resources:
        • Business Sectors: https://www.investopedia.com/terms/p/primary-secondary-tertiary-sectors.asp
        • Sector Examples: https://www.britannica.com/topic/sector-economics
  • Topic: Specialized Government Bodies and Credit Regulations
    • Focus: Sector Education and Training Authorities (SETAs), National Credit Act (NCA).
    • Activities:
      • Research the functions and roles of SETAs in different industries.
      • Analyze the provisions of the National Credit Act and its implications for businesses and consumers.
      • Resources:
        • SETAs: https://www.seta.org.za/
        • National Credit Act: https://www.gov.za/documents/national-credit-act-34-2005
